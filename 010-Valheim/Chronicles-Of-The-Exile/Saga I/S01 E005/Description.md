## 🪓 The Narrative

**[Ulf's Voice]**
The golden ones are captured. The wood-wroth falls to the splintered shield. The shadow of the pines grows long. The exile bleeds but does not break. A bed of straw claims the weary soul.

**[Conrad's Chronicle]**
The air turned cold as I stepped beyond the meadows and into the suffocating grip of the pines. I had spent the morning tending the hearth and home, doubling the spits to prepare for the road, but the forest has a way of draining a man's spirit. I found The Golden Ones humming within the rot of the Echoes of the Past, claiming two queens to bring sweetness to our future halls. A beast of the fields, a boar touched by the Strength of Stars, fell to my blade—a reminder that even with elder wisdom, the wild remains perilous. I felt the Wood-Wroth's blow vibrate through my wooden buckler, a desperate parry that saved my life as my heart hammered against my ribs. With only a sliver of life remaining and the Raven’s shadow overhead, I retreated to a hollowed stone shell of a home. I hammered a roof over the flickering flames to keep the rain from drowning my only warmth. As I laid my head upon the rough straw of my new bed, the smell of damp pine and old dust filled my lungs, a reminder that I am still here, however fragile my thread may be.

**[Grandpa's Legend]**
Well, today was a bit of a wake-up call for this old soul. I started off simple enough, adding a second cooking spit to the fire because keeping the family fed takes more than one piece of meat at a time. I managed to find two Queen Bees by tearing down some old shacks, which is a real blessing for the honey we'll need later. I even had a bit of a scrap with a Greydwarf and managed to time my parry just right with that old wood shield, but the Black Forest nearly got the better of me. My health dropped down to 9 HP because I forgot to keep my stomach full—a bitter lesson I hope you exiles don't have to learn the hard way. Ended the night in a drafty abandoned house, but at least I got a roof over the fire and a bed to rest my aching bones.

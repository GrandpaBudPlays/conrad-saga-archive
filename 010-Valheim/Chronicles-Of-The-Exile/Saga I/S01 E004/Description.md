The scent of fresh-cut pine filled the air as I laid the floorboards of the Othala, building a true hearth and home. I watched the smoke curl upward, a sign of life against the grey sky. When the Wood-Wroth came, sent by The Forsaken, the air grew heavy with ancient malice; my breath came in ragged gasps as my blood stained the grass. But the Norns were not finished with me yet. I found strength in the sizzle of meat over the flames, a bit of elder wisdom keeping me whole. By the time I donned the leather helmet, the world felt a little less vast. The Golden Ones now hum at the edge of the sanctuary, their steady vibration a promise of sweetness. I dismantled the Echoes of the Past, using tools that felt like stone-marrow tools in my weary hands. Now, the Othala is marked, a defiant spark in the dark of the tenth world.

**[Grandpa's Legend]**
Well, kids, Day 6 was a real test of the spirit. We spent the morning getting the 26-degree roof just right on the Othala and building a proper chimney so the smoke wouldn't choke us out. I managed to upgrade our workbench with a new chopping block using 10 wood and 10 flint, which was lucky because Eikthyr sent his forest-kin to tear us down right after. My heart was hammering when my health dropped to 12 HP, but we held the line. By Day 7, I had a new leather helmet on my head and two beehives humming nearby to provide us with some sweetness. It’s a good feeling to see the home finally marked on the map, even if we had to tear down an old abandoned village to get the lumber we needed.

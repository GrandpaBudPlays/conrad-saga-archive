## 🪓 The Narrative

**[Ulf's Voice]**

**[Grandpa's Legend]**
Yup, it was a long trek to the edge of the Whale-road, but finding that second Eikthyr Altar (Altar of the Forsaken) made the journey worth it. I managed to take down a Neck (Scaly-kin) with the Strength of Stars—a two-star little devil—and kept its head as my very first trophy. Between the Echoes of the Past in those abandoned villages, I found some Beehives (The Golden Ones) and built a Cooking Station (The Hearth-Spit) to keep the family fed. I finally sat down to craft a Flint Axe (Stone-biter) to replace my old tool; it cuts much cleaner when you're clearing space for a proper Othala. Now, here's a bit of an old soul's wisdom: always mark your raspberry patches on your map, because a hungry belly makes for a short temper when you're lost in the woods. It felt good to finally lay my head in a Bed (Hearth-rest) and set my roots in this strange land.

**[Conrad's Chronicle]**
The player established a functional base by constructing a workbench, a bed for a spawn point, and a basic emergency shelter. Exploration of the starting island's perimeter led to the discovery of a second Eikthyr altar and the identification of the ocean boundaries. Combat encounters included the elimination of a one-star boar and a two-star neck, which yielded the player's first trophy. Resource gathering was bolstered by hunting deer for leather and locating multiple beehives in an abandoned village. Equipment was upgraded through the crafting of rag trousers and a flint axe, while a chest in an abandoned structure provided gold and flint arrows. Mapping efforts were prioritized, with markers placed for raspberry clusters and the abandoned village site.

The core appeal of this session is the 'Grandpa' authenticity—specifically the moment he realizes he already owns the troll cape he was hunting for. We should lean into the 'honest grind' narrative. ## 🪵 Ledger Entry (Campfire Story)
> Gather 'round the fire, for the Black Forest is a fickle teacher. Today, I set out with a simple plan: clear the inventory and get to work. But the Norns had a blue-skinned giant waiting in the shadows. We danced with that troll until my quiver ran dry, forcing me to fletch wood arrows on the fly just to finish the job. There's a certain irony in hunting a beast for its hide only to realize your old bones are already wearing the very thing you're looking for—aye, memory is the first thing to go in these woods. We spent the rest of the sun's light pulling copper from the belly of the earth. It’s a heavy, honest labor, hauling those stones back to the smelter while the Greydwarfs throw pebbles like petulant children. By the time the moon rose, we had the forge humming. With a bit of fine wood and some fresh bronze, we’ve fashioned a mace that’ll make the draugr think twice. The garden is planted, the hearth is warm, and though the work is slow, the progress is steady. That's the way of the exile.

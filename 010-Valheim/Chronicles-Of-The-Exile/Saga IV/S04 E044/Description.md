The earth is torn to hold the sea. The wild swine bow to the grain. Life blooms in the mud of the new garth. The mountain's cold heart calls to the forge. The warrior prepares for the frost.

**[Conrad's Chronicle]**
The salt spray of the whale-road met the edge of my stone-marrow tools as I carved a moat into the Westshore soil, the earth surrendering to the rising tide. I felt the rough, ancient skin of the Iron-Bark oak as I brought it down to frame the eaves of West-Thwaite, a labor for our hearth and home. Inside the new garth, the air was thick with the scent of pine smoke as I wrestled with the stone-breath of the chimney, minding the Wyrd's weight. The silence was broken by the soft grunting of beast taming—two great souls now joined by three small lives. But Othala bears the scars of the Norns' Trial, its timbers splintered. I planted saplings in the dark loam, a promise to the future, before turning my eyes toward the Shadow-Garth Forge. The siren song of the mountain's Frost-Tears pulls at my soul, a strength of stars calling me to the high peaks.

**[Grandpa's Legend]**
Well, we finally got the West-Thwaite outbuilding looking like a proper home, even if that X-frame chimney gave me a bit of a hitch-in-my-gitty-up with the airflow. It was a bitter lesson seeing the damage the Norns' Trial left on Othala, but seeing those two boars finally settle down and give us three little piglets made the work worth it. I spent the day digging a moat down to sea level to keep the family safe from the wood-wroth. We even got some oak Iron-Bark harvested and new saplings in the dirt. Now, it's time to head to Shadow-Garth Forge; those Frost-Tears won't smelt themselves, and we've got a Silver Age to start.
